How socioeconomic factors decide your health risks?

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

How big is your concern about your health, and what is your estimation of your exposure to health risks? A recent study based on data from the U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System has revealed correlations between age, income, and health risk.

As the diagram demonstrates, people's health risk is shaped by different socioeconomic in different ways. Poverty is shown to correlate with increased health risk exposure positively in all aspects.

Interestingly, age seems to have contrasting impacts on health risk as higher age is positively correlated to better health care access while this appears to lead to increased smoking. Finally, lower-income is also associated with higher health risks.
